#+title: TBI_RacialEthnicMinorities
* Data Supporting the issue/disparity
- American Indian/Alaska Native children and adults have higher rates of TBI-related hospitalizations and deaths than other racial or ethnic groups
- In addition to differences in rates of TBI, racial and ethnic minority groups, particularly non-Hispanic Black and Hispanic patients.
* Contributing Factors
- Factors that contribute to this disparity include higher rates of motor vehicle crashes, substance use, and suicide as well as difficulties in accessing appropriate healthcare.
-  less likely to receive follow-up care and rehabilitation following a TBI compared to non-Hispanic white patients.
* Consequences of TBI to that group
- Racial and ethnic minorities are also more likely to have poor psychosocial, functional, and employment-related outcomes after sustaining a TBI than non-Hispanic white individuals
